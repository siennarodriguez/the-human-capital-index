The Human Capital Index

Human capital consists of the knowledge, skills, and health that people invest in and accumulate throughout their lives, enabling them to realize their potential as members of society. All data for 2020 represent the status of countries pre-COVID-19 (sourced from World Bank Group)

What is the Human Capital Index?

The Human Capital Index (HCI) measures the human capital that a child born today can expect to attain by the age of 18, given the risks of poor health and poor education prevailing in her country. It asks fundamental questions about whether a child will survive, how healthy she will be growing up, and whether she will attain real learning in school.

Health

The Adult Survival Rate is calculated by subtracting the mortality rate for 15-60 year-olds from 1. Mortality rates for 15-60 year-olds are retrieved from the United Nations Population Division World Population Prospects, 2019 data release, supplemented with data provided by World Bank staff. Most recent estimates as of 2019 are used.

Survival

The Probability of Survival to Age 5 is calculated by subtracting the under-5 mortality rate from 1. Under-5 mortality rates are retrieved from the UN Interagency Group for Child Mortality Estimates, September 2019 data release, supplemented with data provided by World Bank staff.

Education

The Expected Years of School is calculated as the sum of age-specific enrollment rates between ages 4 and 17. Age-specific enrollment rates are approximated using school enrollment rates at different levels: pre-primary enrollment rates approximate the age-specific enrolment rates for 4 and 5 year-olds; the primary rate approximates for 6-11 year-olds; the lower-secondary rate approximates for 12-14 year-olds, and the upper-secondary approximates for 15-17 year-olds. Enrollment rates are retrieved from the UNESCO Institute for Statistics, supplemented with data provided by World Bank staff.
