What is the Human Capital Index?

The Human Capital Index (HCI) is an international metric that benchmarks key components of human capital across economies. It measures the human capital that a child born can expect to attain by the age of 18, given the risks of poor health and poor education prevailing in her country.

Health

Will a child leave school in good health, ready for work?

The Adult Survival Rate is calculated by subtracting the mortality rate for 15-60 year-olds from 1. The rates can be interpreted as a proxy for the range of fatal and non-fatal health outcomes that a child born today would experience as an adult if the current conditions prevail into the future.

Survival

Will children born today survive to school age?

The Probability of Survival to Age 5 is calculated by subtracting the under-5 mortality rate from 1. The under-5 mortality rate is the probability of a child born in a specified year dying before reaching the age of 5 if subject to current age-specific mortality rates.

Education

How much school will children complete and how much will they learn?

The Expected Years of School of the HCI captures the number of years of school a child born today can expect to obtain by age 18, given the prevailing pattern of enrollment rates in her country. It is calculated as the sum of enrollment rates between ages 4 and 17.

Where do we go from here?

The COVID-19 pandemic has shown us that urgent actions is needed to protect the progress in improving human capital, especially in poor and vulnerable communities. The HCI provides a high-level view of human capital across countries that can help start new conversations. Proven strategies include enganging the whole of society, identifying cross-secotral synergies, and using data to choose effective strategies. The hope is that by applying these tools, governments can go far toward protecting and rebuilding human capital in the wake of COVID-19.
